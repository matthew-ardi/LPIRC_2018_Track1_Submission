LPIRC CVPR 2018 FAQs

Latest Update: 05/18/2018

 

Track 1

Who can participate?

Anyone above 13 years old who is not from US embargoed countries or affiliated with the organizers or the sponsors (Purdue University, Duke University, University of North Carolina Chapel Hill or employees of Facebook or Google Inc).

What phone will be used in the competition?

The competition will use Pixel 2. Compared to Pixel 1, Pixel 2's big core has better support for quantized models. You may want to check out the sample latency page that shows timing of the sample models on different devices.

Can Track 1 use models that are quantized after training?

Yes. You are welcome to try but keep in mind that the model's accuracy may be quite different. It's recommended to introduce quantization during training. To save time you can start quantized training from the float model you've obtained. Please see our tutorial page for details

How many times can a team submit?

Currently, the site is only allowing you to submit up to 1 per day. we are increasing the cap to 3 per day. The submitted files will be processed every 1 hour before scores are posted.

Will a leader board be provided?

Yes

Track 2

Is Track 2 on-site?

No, it is done using ONLINE SUBMISSION. Please note that Track 2, which is designed for the participants using Caffe2+TX2, is NOT an onsite competition. Track 2 requires the participants to submit their design, starting on May 25 and ending on June 10, so that the track organizer can measure the power consumption and accuracy of the design on a standardized platform. No onsite competition phase is arranged However, if you are using other hardware other than TX2, or using a customized TX2 board, or using tools other than Caffe 2, please participated in track 2 instead. For example, Caffe 1 + TX1, Caffe 2 +TX1, and Caffe 1 + TX2 all go to track 3. But Caffe 2 + TX 2 (standard version) goes to track 2. If you have any questions, if do not hesitate to send email to Prof. Chen (yiran.chen@duke.edu) or Jingchi Zhang (jingchi.zhang@duke.edu).

What is the time limit in Track 2?

10 minutes

How many images in Track 2?

20,000

If a team finished 20,000 images, how can the team reduce energy consumption?

You can reduce the clock frequency to reduce power consumption. You can log out from the server and the power meter stops.

Do we need to retrieve all images before processing?

You decide the sequence. You can retrieve all images before processing. You can retrieve one image and process one image. You can retrieve multiple images and process them.

General FAQ

What is the difference of Track 2 and Track 3?

If you use TX2 and Caffe2, you are in Track 2. Otherwise, you are in Track 3. In Track 3, you can use TX2 (without using Caffe 2), or Caffe2 (without using TX2). You may use TX1 in Track 3. In addition, track 3 has no restriction except that you cannot use TX2 AND Caffe 2 at the same time.

How is the score calculated in Track 2 and 3?

mAP and energy consumption

When can I submit? (Track 1 and 2)

May 25 - June 10

Do we use HTTP protocols? (Track 2 and 3)

Yes

Do we need to retrieve all images before processing?

You decide the sequence. You can retrieve all images before processing. You can retrieve one image and process one image. You can retrieve multiple images and process them.

Is there an advantage of faster processing (more images in 10 minutes)?

A faster solution will likely receive a higher score. Yes, you should make your solution fast.
